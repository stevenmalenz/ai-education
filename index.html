The thesis

Why this program exists

Make people more capable, not more replaceable.

The highest-probability bets are AI transition apprenticeships and learning infrastructure for community colleges and adult learners. This program fuses them: teach practical AI through real work, wrap it in a cohort, and leave every community with reusable infrastructure.

01 · Real work

Apprenticeship, not lecture.

Each learner is paired to a live workflow donated by an employer or community organization — the kind of recurring task AI can quietly reshape inside a job.

12 weeks · curriculum

Readiness → Practice → Portfolio

A cohort path that fits a working life.

Twelve weeks, six hours a week — three of cohort time, three of paired workflow practice. Each week ships a small artifact, so learners leave with proof, not promises.

01Readiness

The AI you already touch

Audit the tools already in your work life. Name what helps, what fakes, what harms.

02Readiness

Prompts as small instruments

Write, test, and version prompts the way a craftsperson sharpens tools.

03Readiness

Reading a model's edges

Where models hallucinate, defer, and break. Calibrated trust as a workplace skill.

04Practice

Choose your workflow

Match to a real, recurring task donated by a partner employer or community org.

05Practice

Map the current process

Document the workflow as it exists. Identify friction, repetition, and judgment calls.

06Practice

First AI-assisted draft

Build the smallest possible AI-assisted version. Compare it against baseline.

07Practice

Human-in-the-loop design

Decide where the human stays in the seat. Make the handoffs explicit and humane.

08Practice

Evaluation & guardrails

Set up checks: accuracy, equity, privacy, escalation. Practice failing safely.

09Practice

Deploy with a peer

Pair-deploy the workflow inside the host organization for two real working weeks.

10Portfolio

Write the implementation guide

Turn the experience into a guide the next learner — or organization — can follow.

11Portfolio

Tell the story honestly

Demo what worked, what didn't, and what you'd never automate. To peers and employers.

12Portfolio

Place into a role pathway

Graduate with a portfolio, a role pathway, and a named local mentor for year one.

FAQ

Before you write to ask

Common questions, honest answers.

If your question is not here, write to the program lead at your nearest pilot site, or open an issue on the toolkit repo.

we read every one.

Who is this program for?+

Working adults, returning students, and frontline teams who want practical AI capability without leaving their job or their community. No prior AI experience is required.

What does it cost a cohort site to run?+

Roughly the staff cost of one half-time program lead for the 12 weeks. The curriculum, rubrics, and toolkit are free and open. Employer workflow donations cover most learner stipends in current pilots.

Is the curriculum tied to a specific AI vendor?+

No. Sessions are written model-agnostic and rotate between hosted assistants and local tools, so learners build durable habits — not vendor muscle memory.

How is data privacy handled in workflow donations?+

The donation kit includes a redaction protocol, a data-use agreement, and a default preference for on-device or vendor-isolated tooling. Apprentices never operate on unredacted real customer data without an employer sign-off and an explicit review.

Can I just take the materials and run it myself?+

Yes. That is the entire point. Fork the toolkit, follow the recruitment playbook, and let the network know so we can connect you to a regional mentor.
