The honest case

"Is AI taking the jobs?" Yes — some. Here's the part that matters more.

We won't pretend otherwise: entry-level work is being reshaped, and the numbers below say so plainly. But replacement is rarer than the headlines, and the market isn't punishing AI — it's rewarding the people who use it. The losses hit the roles AI replaces; the gains go to the people who direct it. The surest way onto the right side of that line is to become one of them — and the evidence says beginners gain the most.

~13% Relative decline in employment for workers aged 22–25 in the most AI-exposed jobs since late 2022 — measured against older, less-exposed workers, not absolute job loss. The honest bad news, first. Stanford Digital Economy Lab · 2025
0.7% of analyzed work skills were rated "very likely" to be fully replaced by generative AI. Human oversight stays essential across the board. Indeed Hiring Lab · 2025
56% premium Wage premium for U.S. workers with AI skills over similar roles without them — more than double a year earlier, in every industry analyzed. PwC 2025 Global AI Jobs Barometer
+34% beginners Productivity gain for novice and low-skilled support agents using AI (vs. +14% on average), with little effect on experts. AI helps the least experienced get good fastest. NBER · Brynjolfsson, Li, Raymond · 2023
Why now

The demand is here. The teaching hasn't caught up. That gap is the opening.

Employers aren't asking colleges to police AI — they're asking for graduates who can use it. That demand is moving into entry-level roles right now, and most programs haven't kept up. The space between what students need and what they're getting is exactly what these 12 weeks are built to fill.

12 weeks · curriculum

Readiness → Practice → Portfolio

Twelve weeks that fit a real life — and end with something you can show.

Twelve weeks, six hours a week — three with your cohort, three on a project that's yours. Each week you ship one small thing, so you build proof as you go instead of cramming at the end. No prior AI experience needed. The room is full of people exactly where you are — and the evidence says beginners gain the most.

The AI you already touch

Reading a model's edges

Where models hallucinate, defer, and break. Calibrated trust as a workplace skill.

Choose your project

Pick a real task you care about — the kind of thing you'd actually want to make easier.

05Practice

Map the current process

Document the workflow as it exists. Identify friction, repetition, and judgment calls.

First AI-assisted draft

Build the smallest possible AI-assisted version. Compare it against baseline.

Demo what worked, what didn't, and what you'd never automate. To peers and employers.

FAQ

Before you write to ask

Common questions, honest answers.

If your question isn't here, write to the instructor running your cohort, or open an issue on the kit repo.

Isn't this just teaching me to cheat?+

No. The whole program is about judgment — when to use AI, when not to, and how to check its work. That's a skill employers pay a premium for, not a shortcut. It's exactly why teachers are moving from banning AI to teaching it.

What's the catch — is it really free?+

Really free, and forkable on purpose. It's run by an instructor, not a vendor, and it works even if you never claim a single sponsor credit. Big Tech can chip in credits and guest teachers, but they don't own the program, the curriculum, or your data.

If AI is taking entry-level jobs, why would learning AI help?+

Because the decline hits the roles AI replaces, not the people who direct it. The market pays a 56% premium for AI skills, and the biggest gains go to beginners. Learning it well is how you get on the right side of that line. See the evidence.

I'm already drowning — do I have time for this?+

Six hours a week for 12 weeks, and you finish with a portfolio piece for the job search. It's not extra homework — it's the work the job market is already asking you to show.

Is this only for CS or tech students?+

No. The biggest measured gains show up across fields — support agents, students, writers — not just developers. No coding background and no connections required.

Does it lock me into one AI company?+

No. The program works with any AI tool and rotates between them, so you build skills that last instead of habits tied to one product.

How is my data handled?+

Default to redacting anything sensitive and using privacy-protective tools. You never work on real personal data without an explicit sign-off and review.

Can I just take the materials and run it myself?+

Yes. That's the entire point. Fork the kit, follow the playbook, and let the network know so we can connect you with others running it.
