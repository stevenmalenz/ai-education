# Employer Apprenticeship Playbook

## Purpose

This playbook helps employers and community partners define practical AI-enabled roles around real workflows. The goal is not to hire "AI experts" for every function. The goal is to grow workers who can use AI responsibly inside everyday roles.

## Good Apprenticeship Projects

A good project is:

- Real: It improves a workflow someone already cares about.
- Safe: It avoids private data and high-stakes decisions in the pilot.
- Measurable: It has a before and after.
- Reviewable: A manager or stakeholder can judge quality.
- Repeatable: The learner can document the process for someone else.

Strong examples:

- Intake triage for a nonprofit resource desk
- Meeting note summary and follow-up process
- Plain-language rewrite of public materials
- Grant opportunity research workflow
- Customer FAQ update process
- Student services resource map
- Content calendar draft and review workflow
- Internal standard operating procedure cleanup
- Local market research summary

Avoid in the first cohort:

- Medical, legal, financial, or eligibility decisions
- Unapproved sensitive data
- Fully automated outreach
- Surveillance or worker scoring
- Anything where a mistake could materially harm a person

## Role Brief Template

Role title:

AI-enabled [operations / advising / marketing / data / nonprofit capacity] apprentice

Purpose:

Improve recurring work with AI-assisted drafting, research, summarization, planning, documentation, or analysis while keeping human review in place.

Core tasks:

- Map an existing workflow
- Draft or structure first-pass outputs
- Check claims and sources
- Document prompts and review criteria
- Escalate uncertain or high-risk outputs
- Present before/after evidence

Manager commitments:

- Provide one workflow
- Attend one scoping call
- Give midpoint feedback
- Review the demo
- Discuss possible next role or apprenticeship continuation

Guardrails:

- No final decisions without human approval
- No private or sensitive data in unauthorized tools
- Source checks for factual claims
- Clear disclosure when AI materially shapes an output
- Escalation when uncertain

## Manager Check-In Questions

Week 4:

- Does the learner understand the workflow?
- Have they named the right risks?
- Is the task scoped safely?

Week 8:

- Is the AI-assisted process useful enough to keep testing?
- Are approval points clear?
- What quality bar must the learner meet?

Week 10:

- What changed in time, quality, clarity, or access?
- What should not be scaled?
- What role could use this capability?

Week 12:

- Would you sponsor a next-step project, internship, apprenticeship, or role interview?
- What should the next cohort learn from this project?

## Apprenticeship Success Measures

- Workflow cycle time
- Error reduction
- Quality review score
- Stakeholder usefulness
- Learner confidence
- Manager confidence
- Documentation quality
- Role continuation or interview
