# OpenAI Meeting Brief

## One-Sentence Frame

AI Capability Commons is a ready-to-run, 12-week community program that turns AI access into practical workplace capability for adult learners and frontline institutions.

## Meeting Posture

This is not a funding pitch. The ask is acceleration:

- Design review
- Tool alignment
- Pilot introductions
- Evidence feedback

## Problem

AI tools are spreading faster than trusted local institutions can redesign learning, workforce, and apprenticeship pathways. The result is uneven access, shallow tool use, and a widening gap between people who can turn AI into capability and people who cannot.

## Solution

A modular cohort that combines:

- AI literacy
- Guided learning
- Source-grounded practice
- Real employer or nonprofit workflows
- Portfolio assessment
- Apprenticeship handoff

## Default Pilot

- 30 learners
- 12 weeks
- 3 project partners
- 2 facilitators
- 1 demo day
- 1 public cohort report

## Success Measures

- 80% or higher cohort completion.
- 70% or higher learners demonstrate verification and disclosure competence.
- Each learner completes one reviewed portfolio project.
- Three partner workflows produce before/after evidence.
- At least two employers or community partners identify next-step role pathways.
- Public playbook update released within 10 days of demo day.

## OpenAI Asks

1. Design review: review curriculum, AI tool layer, and measurement plan.
2. Tool alignment: advise on ChatGPT Study Mode, ChatGPT Edu, OpenAI Academy, or nonprofit-safe deployment patterns.
3. Pilot introductions: connect to community-based organizations, education partners, or People-First AI Fund grantees.
4. Evidence partnership: advise what evidence would be most useful after two cohorts.

## Meeting Close

We are starting with or without a check. The question is whether OpenAI wants to help make this a model other communities can copy.
