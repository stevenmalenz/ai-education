# AI Capability Commons Facilitator Guide

## Facilitator Stance

Adults do not need a hype lecture. They need respect, useful practice, room for doubt, and enough structure to leave with evidence of capability.

Default stance:

- Start from learners' existing expertise.
- Make AI practical before making it abstract.
- Normalize uncertainty.
- Teach verification every week.
- Treat accessibility and inclusion as part of quality.
- Keep the program connected to real work.

## Room Norms

Use these in Week 1 and revisit when needed:

- We learn in public, but we do not expose private data.
- We can be excited and skeptical at the same time.
- We do not shame people for not knowing tools.
- We show our work.
- We verify before sharing.
- We keep humans responsible for decisions that affect people.
- We document useful failures.

## Default Session Flow

Each 90-minute session:

1. Opening check-in: 5 minutes
2. Concept and example: 15 minutes
3. Live demonstration: 15 minutes
4. Guided practice: 25 minutes
5. Pair feedback: 15 minutes
6. Portfolio artifact planning: 10 minutes
7. Exit ticket: 5 minutes

Optional 60-minute lab:

- 10 minutes: Q&A
- 35 minutes: individual or pair practice
- 15 minutes: facilitator feedback

## Facilitation Moves

When a learner is anxious:

- Ask what part feels risky.
- Separate job fear from tool confusion.
- Give a small task with a quick win.
- Pair them with someone patient.

When a learner over-trusts AI:

- Ask what would happen if the answer were wrong.
- Require a source check.
- Ask them to produce a "known, unknown, needs human review" list.

When a learner says the output is good enough:

- Ask who will use it.
- Ask what the user needs to trust it.
- Ask what would make it more accessible.

When a learner wants to automate too much:

- Identify the approval point.
- Identify the reversal point.
- Identify the person harmed if the automation fails.

## Feedback Pattern

Use "Keep, Check, Change":

- Keep: What is working and should remain.
- Check: What needs verification, human review, or more context.
- Change: What should be revised before use.

## Accessibility Defaults

- Provide materials before sessions.
- Use plain-language instructions.
- Read key prompts aloud and show them visually.
- Make all activities possible in pairs.
- Avoid speed contests.
- Offer examples from multiple sectors.
- Ask learners what access supports they need.

## Demo Day Format

Each learner gets five minutes:

1. Who the workflow serves
2. What the old process looked like
3. Where AI helped
4. How the learner checked quality and risk
5. What changed
6. What should happen next

Panel feedback:

- One strength
- One risk or open question
- One role or next-step suggestion

## Facilitator Prep Checklist

Before the cohort:

- Confirm tool access.
- Confirm privacy rules.
- Prepare three sample workflows.
- Collect learner access needs.
- Confirm project partners.
- Create portfolio folders.
- Create baseline survey.

Before each session:

- Prepare one practical demo.
- Prepare one low-risk practice dataset or scenario.
- Prepare one extension task for advanced learners.
- Prepare one plain-language recap.

After each session:

- Review exit tickets.
- Identify learners needing support.
- Update the local playbook.
- Capture useful examples and failures.
