# AI Tool Layer

## Principle

Use AI as a learning companion, not an answer machine.

The cohort teaches every learner to repeat five moves:

1. Ask: turn a work goal into a clear question with context, constraints, and success criteria.
2. Guide: use AI to ask questions, break down steps, and expose thinking instead of skipping to the answer.
3. Make: create a draft, checklist, memo, quiz, workflow, visual explanation, or practice scenario.
4. Check: verify factual claims, compare against trusted sources, test edge cases, and mark uncertainty.
5. Show: explain what AI helped with, what the learner changed, and where human judgment stays responsible.

## Default Tool Stack

- ChatGPT or Gemini for guided practice.
- NotebookLM or equivalent source-grounded workspace for readings, policies, and project sources.
- Google Docs, Sheets, Microsoft 365, or equivalent for portfolio artifacts.
- LMS, shared folder, or printed packet for session materials.
- Survey form for baseline, exit tickets, and outcome measures.

## Weekly Integration

Weeks 0-2:

- Guided-learning chat.
- Learner output: AI use statement and prompt pattern library.
- Control: no sensitive data; facilitator reviews first prompts.

Weeks 3-4:

- Source-grounded notebook.
- Learner output: verification checklist and workflow map.
- Control: trusted source list and citation requirement.

Weeks 5-7:

- Multimodal learning.
- Learner output: workflow card and inclusive use review.
- Control: accessibility check and audience review.

Weeks 8-10:

- Project co-pilot.
- Learner output: prototype, test notes, before/after evidence.
- Control: approval gates, reversibility, and "do not automate" list.

Weeks 11-12:

- Portfolio coach.
- Learner output: demo, role brief, and next-step plan.
- Control: disclosure statement and stakeholder panel review.

## Learner Prompt: Guide Me

You are my learning coach for this task. Do not give me the final answer first.

Ask one question at a time. Help me explain what I already know, identify what I need to check, and decide the next step.

When I make a claim, ask how I know. When I get stuck, give a small hint, not the whole solution.

Task:
[describe the task]

Context:
[add role, audience, constraints, sources, and what success looks like]

## Learner Prompt: Source-Grounded Study

Use only the sources I provide unless I ask you to search.

Create:

1. A plain-language explanation
2. Five flashcards
3. A short quiz
4. Three likely misconceptions
5. A checklist I can use at work

For every factual claim, point back to the source section. Mark anything uncertain.
