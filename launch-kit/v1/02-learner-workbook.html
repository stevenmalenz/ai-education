Portfolio workspace

Learner Workbook

Learners can use this as a printed workbook, copied Google Doc, or LMS module. Each week creates one piece of portfolio evidence.

Your promise to yourself

By the end of this cohort, you will be able to show a real workflow you improved, explain how you used AI, prove how you checked the work, and name what should stay human.

Weekly workbook

WeekLearning goalArtifactChallenge
Week 1AI, Agency, and the Work Ahead
Learners can explain where AI helps, where it fails, and where human judgment remains necessary.		Personal AI use statement.Ask an AI tool to explain a task you already know. Mark what is useful, shallow, wrong, and missing.
Week 2Prompting as Work Design
Learners can turn vague requests into clear AI-assisted work briefs.		Prompt pattern library.Rewrite three weak prompts into useful briefs using context, task, constraints, example, and review criteria.
Week 3Verification and Source Discipline
Learners can check factual claims, mark uncertainty, and decide when not to use AI.		Verification checklist.Have AI summarize a public article, then compare the output to the source and correct errors.
Week 4Workflow Mapping
Learners can map a recurring workflow and identify safe opportunities for AI assistance.		Workflow map and risk notes.Map one recurring workflow with inputs, steps, decisions, handoffs, risks, and outputs.
Week 5Role Lab: Communication and Operations
Learners can use AI to draft, revise, summarize, and organize without losing accountability.		Operations workflow card.Convert messy notes into an action summary with owners, dates, open questions, and risks.
Week 6Role Lab: Data, Research, and Decisions
Learners can structure messy information and produce decision support with caveats.		Evidence memo.Compare three options for a real decision and document what still needs human review.
Week 7Accessibility and Inclusion by Default
Learners can improve readability, accessibility, and inclusion in AI-assisted work.		Inclusive use review.Revise one document for plain language, screen reader structure, and multiple reading levels.
Week 8Automation with Guardrails
Learners can distinguish assistive AI from delegated automation and design human review points.		Guardrail plan.Design a low-risk automation and explain where humans remain in the loop.
Week 9Portfolio Sprint One
Learners can define a portfolio project with user, problem, baseline, desired state, and constraints.		Project brief and first prototype.Build the smallest useful AI-assisted version of the workflow.
Week 10Portfolio Sprint Two
Learners can test, revise, and document before-and-after evidence.		Test notes and before/after evidence.Run two tests: one quality test and one usefulness test.
Week 11Story, Demo, and Role Pathway
Learners can tell the story honestly and connect the work to a role pathway.		Demo script, role brief, and portfolio narrative.Deliver a five-minute demo that includes what worked, what failed, and what should stay human.
Week 12Demo Day and Next Cohort Handoff
Learners graduate with proof, feedback, and a named next step.		Final portfolio, public share post, and next-step plan.Publish or privately share the portfolio with a clear disclosure and ask.

Reflection pages

Before each session

  • What did I try since last time?
  • Where did I get stuck?
  • What do I need from the room today?

After each session

  • What changed in my understanding?
  • What did I verify?
  • What will I add to my portfolio folder?

For every AI output

  • What prompt did I use?
  • What source or context did I provide?
  • What did I check?
  • What did I change before using it?

Before demo day

  • What was the old workflow?
  • Where did AI help?
  • What evidence changed?
  • What would I never automate?
  • What next role or opportunity does this support?
