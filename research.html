What the evidence says to build

Design logic

Two bets become one operating model.

Apprenticeships make the learning real. Adult-learning infrastructure makes it distributable. The Commons ties them together with a shared curriculum, local host paths, and a measurement plan.

01

Ask

Learners use AI to ask better questions, expose assumptions, and define a real workflow.

02

Guide

AI acts as coach, tutor, and critic, pushing productive struggle instead of answer copying.

03

Make

Every learner builds artifacts: process maps, prototypes, tests, demos, and role briefs.

04

Check

Outputs are verified against sources, stakeholder needs, privacy rules, and escalation paths.

05

Show

Graduates explain what worked, what failed, what saved time, and what should stay human.

Evidence program

A 10/10 version produces evidence other communities can use.

The first two cohorts should prove capability gain, learning quality, work usefulness, and pathway conversion. After that, the program can support matched-site research with a college or lab partner.

01

Capability gain

Pre/post checks on task definition, guided prompting, source checking, uncertainty, and explanation.

02

Learning quality

Delayed recall, misconception checks, quiz scores, confidence movement, and facilitator notes.

03

Work usefulness

Hours saved, quality review, accessibility improvement, cycle-time change, and partner usefulness score.

04

Pathway conversion

Interviews, apprenticeships, internships, promotions, mentor matches, and employer repeat interest.

Adult learning defaults

What makes the curriculum work

Respect constraints. Build confidence through real work.

The curriculum avoids treating adults like passive course completers. It uses scenario practice, mentorship, source-grounded tools, local projects, and human judgment as core learning outcomes.
